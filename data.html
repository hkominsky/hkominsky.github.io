Interactive Overview of Food Rating vs Tuition Increase

First, we have an interactive overview comparing the food rating - a standardized review of their dining hall quality with a letter grade ranging from D-A+ - of an institution to the change in their tuition fees over a five year period. What we found is that overall, there is no significant correlation between food rating and tuition increases over a five year period. For instance, insitutions with a D rating experienced average tuition increases of 4.5k over five years, while those with a C, B, A, and A+ rating saw a 3.8k, 4.2k, 4.3k, and 4.6k increase respectivelly. So while the insitutions with the highest food rating did see the greatest tuition jump, there was no significant trend in the variation between the different grades.

Overall, this indicates that other factors are more important in the determinance of a good food score, which can include overall tuition, location, or student population over tuition change over a five year time period in particular.
