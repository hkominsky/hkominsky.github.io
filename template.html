About Me

Hello and welcome to my website! My name is Hugh Kominsky, and I am a fourth-year computer science and finance student at Northeastern University. These majors came to me naturally due to my love of complex and creative problem-solving. I am particularly interested in the intersection of computer science applications in the financial sector. To highlight this interest, this website will detail more about my interest and how it was fostered in the first place. Naturally being interested in a new and ever-changing field, I have needed to learn more and acquire technical skills. To this end, I have had numerous work experiences in college that have allowed me to expand my horizons in computer science and finance. I recently landed my second and final co-op at Scotiabank as an Equity Derivatives Quantitative Developer in New York City for the spring 2025 cycle. Before that, I had my first co-op at Marsh McLennan as a Catastrophe Analyst, where I aided insurance companies find reinsurance coverage for their insurance policies. Lastly, I also worked as a data engineering intern at J&K Seminars in Wyncote, PA where I was involved with modernizing their database architecture and visualization efforts. Through my past and future experiences, I hope to gain more experience in the fields I love and continually learn more.
